Iran & United States Relations

A Comprehensive Timeline of Interaction, Conflict, and Diplomacy

Historical Milestones

1953: The Coup

The CIA and UK intelligence back a coup to overthrow Iran's democratically elected Prime Minister, Mohammad Mosaddegh, reinstating the Shah to protect Western oil interests.

1979: Islamic Revolution & Hostage Crisis

The Shah is overthrown, and Ayatollah Khomeini takes power. In November, radical students storm the US Embassy in Tehran, holding 52 Americans hostage for 444 days.

2015: The JCPOA (Nuclear Deal)

Iran and world powers (led by the US) sign a landmark deal to limit Iran's nuclear program in exchange for sanctions relief. The US later withdrew in 2018 under the Trump administration.

The 2026 Conflict (Operation Epic Fury)

Key Update: On February 28, 2026, the US and Israel launched joint airstrikes targeting Iranian military infrastructure and leadership.
  • February 28, 2026: Start of "Operation Epic Fury." High-ranking Iranian officials were targeted in initial waves.
  • Iranian Retaliation: Iran responded with hundreds of drone and ballistic missile strikes against US bases in the region and shipping in the Strait of Hormuz.
  • Global Impact: The conflict led to a significant disruption in global oil trade and energy prices.

Current Status: Two-Week Ceasefire

As of April 8, 2026, a fragile two-week ceasefire has been agreed upon by the United States, Iran, and Israel.

Mediator: Pakistan & International Diplomats
Key US Demand: Permanent opening of the Strait of Hormuz and total halt of uranium enrichment.
Key Iran Demand: Full sanctions relief and guarantees against future military strikes.

Diplomatic efforts are currently underway to transform this temporary truce into a long-term framework for regional stability.
